Project escalation

Lifelike NPCs

Fear response system

"Reputation""


The value that npcs use to guage how much of a threat the player is to their specific realm, community, or group. This determines the measures they will take to stop the threat, as well as their willingness to evacuate should the threat be spotted nearby.

The Vision

The Problem

Honor Systems and morality meters often fail to produce consequences that lead to players rethinking their descisions. NPCs are not equipped to coordinate and react properly to large scale acts of violence from the player. Killing an entire village may only lead to a thematic slap on the wrist or an npc expressing mild discomfort with the player's actions. This lack of initiative from the characters in the game world to properly deal with a rising threat can create a break in immersion for the player.

The Solution

Project Escalation intends to fix this problem by creating a fear based reaction system rather than a moral one. Instead of quantifying the ethics of a players actions, the game will quantify the threat level or "Reputation" a player has based on it's feats. Civilian NPCs may chose to hide or run away upon sight of a particularly bloodthirsty player. Militant factions will coordinate bigger response teams depending on the amount of damage a player has proven themselves capable of doing.'.

Possible Effects

Players may think more carefully before burning down entire villages and killing random NPCs. The system idealy will not act as a punishment for these actions but rather makes the considerations behind commiting them more interesting.
